SafetyDrone FAQ

The answers below cover the most common questions we get from public safety UAS teams about SafetyDrone—how we store data, which drones and apps we support, and what happens to your account over time.

Data Storage Privacy and Security
Where/how is my data stored?
All SafetyDrone data—mission records, flight logs, documents, and user information—is stored on secure cloud infrastructure with regular backups. Our environment uses SOC 2–aligned security controls, encryption in transit and at rest, and modern access management so only authorised users in your organisation can see it.
What do you do with my data?
We do not sell or share your personal data or mission data. That includes contact information (such as email addresses) and UAS telemetry. We may look at anonymised, aggregate statistics (for example, which drone models are most popular) to guide product development, but we do not expose identifiable agency or mission details.
Who owns the data in my account?
Your agency retains ownership of its operational and training data. SafetyDrone provides the platform that stores, organises, and displays it so you can meet internal policy, regulatory, and public transparency requirements.
Product Support
How can I upgrade my SafetyDrone plan?
To move to a higher plan, first cancel your current paid subscription from your account settings. We will rebate the prorated balance of your existing plan, and you can then start a new subscription on the updated plan level.
What happens to my data when my subscription expires?
If your subscription expires, you lose access to the paid features of that plan—but your data is not deleted. Your agency’s missions, logs, and documents remain safely stored. When you reactivate or upgrade, your access is restored.
How often do you add new features?
We release improvements regularly based on feedback from police, fire, EMS, SAR, and emergency management users. New capabilities, integrations, and workflow updates are announced through our release notes and updates, so you can quickly see what’s changed and how it benefits your UAS program.
Can I send ideas or feedback?
Absolutely. SafetyDrone is shaped by real-world public safety experience. If you have ideas to improve checklists, mission workflows, integration support, or reporting, go to the Contact Us page and send us a note—select “Feedback” or “Technical Issue” as the category.
Can I contact you with ideas to improve the service?
You, the drone pilot, provide real-world experience to help us improve the logbook functionality and workflow. We always welcome your feedback. Click the “Contact Us” tab to send us a note.
What drones / software do you support?
We support log files and telemetry from over 50 suppliers including DJI, Parrot, Autel, Wingtra, Yuneec, Wingcopter, SenseFly, Flyability, Vantage Robotics, 3DR (Pixhawk), DroneDeploy, Pix4d, DroneLink, DroneHarmony, Autopilot (Auto Flight Logic) and many more. SafetyDrone Mobile Apps

SafetyDrone runs on secure cloud infrastructure with a modern web app and dedicated mobile applications. Crews can:

  • • Access missions from the station, EOC, or field
  • • Complete checklists and forms on mobile devices
  • • Log flights offline and sync when connectivity returns
  • • See current airspace status, local weather, and solar-activity readings that may affect flight safety


Our additional Android app, DLBSync, pulls flights from major mobile flight control apps directly into your SafetyDrone account. It can collect logs even in low-coverage environments and upload them to the cloud once Wi-Fi or mobile data is available—reducing manual export steps and helping ensure your incident and training records are complete.
