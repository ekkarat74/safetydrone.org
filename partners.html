UAV Industry Integration for Public Safetyn

SafetyDrone is built to plug into the public safety drone ecosystem, not lock you into a single vendor. We work with any UAV / UAS hardware or software supplier that your agency relies on—Skydio, Autel, Wingtra, FLIR, mapping tools, flight-control apps, you name it—to capture mission data automatically and turn it into useful, searchable records.

Using our flight log import tools, APIs, and web services, agencies can integrate SafetyDrone with their existing tech stack to reduce manual data entry, tighten workflows, and improve return on investment (ROI) for every airframe, battery, and software license. As new drones and apps enter the public safety market, we extend our integrations so your program can evolve without changing platforms. Safety Drone

Our Key Partners

Skydio
Skydio is a U.S.-based drone manufacturer and a global leader in autonomous flight. Their aircraft combine advanced onboard AI with robust airframes to support demanding public safety, enterprise, and government missions. SafetyDrone integration allows Skydio flight data to flow directly into your agency’s logbook, simplifying compliance, training analysis, and incident review. Safety Drone
Autel Robotics
Autel Robotics provides versatile aerial systems widely used by public safety, government, and commercial operators. With operations across multiple regions, Autel platforms are a popular choice for agencies seeking flexible, high-performance drones. SafetyDrone captures and organises Autel flight logs so your team can manage operations, maintenance, and reporting from one system. Safety Drone
Wingtra
Wingtra manufactures the WingtraOne VTOL mapping drone, designed to collect high-precision aerial data over large areas. Its hybrid VTOL/fixed-wing design is ideal for disaster assessment, search grids, and infrastructure mapping. When paired with SafetyDrone, Wingtra flight data is imported and stored alongside your multirotor missions, giving you one continuous history of public safety UAV activity. Safety Drone

SafetyDrone Mobile Apps

The SafetyDrone platform runs on secure cloud infrastructure with a modern web application and companion mobile apps so your crews can work from the station, the EOC, or the field. Use the app for offline access, then sync missions back to your cloud account when connectivity returns.


The mobile tools provide quick access to current airspace status, local weather, and solar index readings that may affect flight safety. Our additional Android app, DLBSync, pulls flights from major mobile flight-control apps directly into your SafetyDrone account, even when operating in low-coverage areas—helping you capture more complete records with less manual effort.
